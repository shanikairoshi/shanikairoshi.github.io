Research identity

My PhD develops adaptive federated learning and quantum federated learning methods that respond to real training diagnostics rather than relying only on fixed optimisation rules. The research connects theoretical modelling, algorithm design, quantum-aware aggregation, benchmark evaluation, and reusable scientific software.

Main PhD contributions

Deep-unfolded adaptive optimisation

Learnable round-wise control mechanisms for federated learning, replacing static hyperparameters with diagnostic-driven adaptation.

Quantum federated learning

Adaptive QFL methods for variational quantum models, shot-limited training, parameter-shift behaviour, circuit controls, and quantum noise settings.

Practical aggregation constraints

Fidelity-aware, latency-aware, instability-aware, and geometry-aware aggregation strategies for distributed and quantum-assisted learning.

DeepUnfolding-Agent API

A reusable FastAPI platform for controller creation, diagnostics, control suggestion, feedback update, and benchmark workflows.

Reproducible research map

Chapter-by-chapter links to papers, code, datasets, figures, benchmark scripts, and citation material.

Real-world relevance

Evaluation across healthcare, genomics, finance, transport, and intelligent systems.
