PhD: Modeling Quantum Federated Aggregation

This page presents the PhD topic, research problem, thesis contributions, chapter structure, and links to papers, software, datasets, and reproducibility artefacts.

Research problem

Federated learning enables collaborative model training without centralising raw data. However, many existing methods rely on fixed aggregation and optimisation rules. These fixed rules can become unstable under non-IID data, client drift, noisy updates, limited communication, quantum measurement noise, and heterogeneous execution settings.

Thesis aim

The thesis aims to develop adaptive and quantum-aware federated learning frameworks that can learn control behaviour across training rounds and expose these models through reusable software interfaces.
